# LinkedIn: voice and format

**Logic of the platform for this launch:** LinkedIn is not where the kit gets adopted; it is where the builder gets evaluated. The reader who matters is a staff engineer or hiring manager at an AI-native company. Every post is written to survive being read aloud in an interview. The validation-rigor-applied-to-AI angle lives here and only here (it is the founder's story, not the product's headline).

Format rules:
- Full prose paragraphs, 4-6 of them. No one-line-paragraph stacks, no rhetorical-question openers, none of the banned cadence tics from the GUIDELINES ban list, even though the platform rewards all of them. <!-- voice-lint:allow -->
- Each post contains at least one disclosed failure or unproven claim, stated before the reader could find it. On this platform the failures do more work than the features.
- First person singular throughout. Employer names appear only as biography (Zebra), never as endorsement.
- No hashtags in the post body; LinkedIn's reach math does not need them and the audience discounts them. No emoji. No exclamation marks. No em dashes.
- One repo link per post, at the end, in plain text.
- Strip the bracketed claim IDs before posting.

Cadence: one post per week for four weeks (calendar has the first two). Never post the same day as an X thread; each LinkedIn post is written fresh for this audience rather than reflowed from the X package (shared maxims like "an alarm that always fires is no alarm" may recur; shared paragraphs may not).
